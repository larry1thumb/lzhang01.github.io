Kirkpatrick-Seidel's Prune-and-Search Convex Hull Algorithm

Introduction

In Computational Geometry, a convex hull of a set X of points in a Euclidean plane is the smallest number of points that contain all points of X.

Example of a convex hull of a point set

Prior to the Ultimate Convex Hull Algorithm by Kirkpatrick and Seidel, most convex hull algorithms relied on sorting, making each algorithm take at least O(n log n) time. Two output sensitive algorithms, the Jarvis March and Quickhull had time complexities of O(nh) with h being the number of points on the convex hull, however in the worst case these algorithms can have O(n2)

With the Ultimate Convex Hull Algorithm, we have a time complexity of O(n log h). Though it is also an output sensitive algorithm, its worst case is asymptotically to the sorting algorithms.
